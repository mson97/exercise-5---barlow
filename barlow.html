Barlow

Barlow was designed by Jeremy Tribby in San Francisco, California.
Barlow was inspired by the look of California street signs, public transportation, and license plates.
It comes in three widths and nine different weights, making it extremely versatile.
Thus, Barlow can is suitable for large or small texts and in digial or print forms.
We aren't quite sure what websites use Barlow as it was released fairly recently.
One website that uses Barlow is the creator's demo website for the font.
Check it out!
From the website's interactive specimen, you can view the many possible iterations of Barlow.

What we like best about Barlow is how thin and readable it is.
It's a sans-serif font that is both clean and simple, making it suitable for professional and casual uses.
There is ample spacing within and between letters, making it very pleasing to the eye.
We do think that Barlow is quite plain and is not attention grabbing.
It is quite similar to other existing fonts such as Roboto or Open Sans.
Our least favorite thing about Barlow is the ugly capital Q.
Barlow is definitely has a minimalistic, simple, and modern personality.
Barlow does not have an extensive history of usage due to how new it is.
However, an interesting fact about this font is that it is named after John Perry Barlow, a pioneer in the internet.
